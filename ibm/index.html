📖 Today's Flip  ·  Course 5 — Deep Learning with PyTorch In Progress
Before you watch, think about…
  • 1 You've built a logistic regression model in PyTorch — one linear layer plus sigmoid. What do you think happens when you stack two or three of those layers? What new capability does depth give you?
  • 2 In C4 you used MSE loss for regression. Classification predicts a category, not a number. Why do you think MSE isn't the right choice — and what property should a classification loss have?
  • 3 CNNs dominate image tasks. A plain neural network could technically process pixel values too. What do you think CNNs do differently that makes them so much better at spatial data?
Watch for…
How activation functions (especially ReLU) are what allow deep networks to learn non-linear decision boundaries — without them, stacking layers is mathematically equivalent to one layer. Also watch for softmax: the multi-class generalisation of sigmoid, and how cross-entropy loss is designed to punish confident wrong answers far more than MSE ever could.
