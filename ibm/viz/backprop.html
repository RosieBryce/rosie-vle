What to Notice
  • The chain rule in action: Every gradient flows backward through the network, multiplied at each step by the local derivative (sigmoid′ = a(1−a)). This is the chain rule applied to a composition of functions.
  • Sigmoid saturation damping: When z is large (a → 1 or a → 0), σ′(z) = a(1−a) → 0. The gradient shrinks as it passes through a saturated neuron. This is the vanishing gradient problem — worse with many layers.
  • The gradient is the slope: dL/dw₁ tells you: if w₁ increases by a tiny amount, how much does the loss change? A large gradient means a steep slope — take a big step. A small gradient means the loss is already near flat.
  • After "Run 100 Epochs": Watch the loss curve decrease and then flatten. The rate of decrease depends on α (learning rate). With α = 0.5, the network converges in ~50 epochs for these default values.
